**A/B Testing** lets you run controlled experiments on your campaign messages. Instead of guessing which wording, tone, or call-to-action will perform best, you can create multiple variants, split traffic between them, and use real results to choose a winner.

This feature lives inside the **Proactive AI > Engagement** section and works alongside your existing Campaigns workflow.

<Info>
  A/B Testing is designed for outbound campaign messages. Make sure you have at least one active campaign or message template before creating a test.
</Info>

---

## Key Concepts

### Tests

An A/B test is a container that holds two or more **variants** of a message. Each test has:

- **Name** — a descriptive label so your team can identify the experiment (e.g. `Welcome offer – Jan 2025`)
- **Status** — the current state of the test:
  - **Draft** — created but not yet running
  - **Running** — actively sending variants to contacts
  - **Completed** — finished, either manually stopped or after a winner was selected
- **Winner** — the variant you (or your team) select as the best performer

### Variants

Each variant represents a different version of the message you want to test. A variant includes:

- **Name** — a short label (e.g. `Variant A`, `Friendly tone`)
- **Message** — the actual message content sent to contacts
- **Traffic percentage** — the share of your audience that will receive this variant

<Note>
  Traffic percentages across all variants in a test should add up to **100 %**.
</Note>

## Creating an A/B Test

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to A/B Testing">
    Go to **Proactive AI > Engagement > A/B Testing** in the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click New A/B Test">
    Click the **New A/B Test** button in the page header to start creating your experiment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name your test">
    Give the test a clear, descriptive name so your team can find it later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add variants">
    Create two or more variants. For each one, enter:
    - A **variant name**
    - The **message content**
    - The **traffic percentage** this variant should receive
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch the test">
    Once you're happy with the setup, start the test. Its status will change to **Running** and messages will begin going out.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing a Running Test

While a test is running you can:

- **Select a winner** — Click **Select as Winner** on the variant that is performing best. The test will be marked as **Completed** and the winning variant will display a **Winner** badge.
- **Stop the test** — Click **Stop Test** to end the experiment immediately. The test status changes to **Completed** without selecting a winner.

<Tip>
  Let the test run long enough to gather meaningful data before choosing a winner. Small sample sizes can lead to misleading results.
</Tip>

---

## Best Practices

- **Test one variable at a time.** Change only the message wording, or only the call-to-action—not both. This makes it easier to understand what drove the difference.
- **Balance your traffic.** A 50/50 split is the simplest starting point for two variants. Use uneven splits only when you want to limit exposure to an untested idea.
- **Document your learnings.** After completing a test, note what worked and apply those insights to future campaigns.
- **Combine with Campaigns.** Use the winning variant as the template for your next full campaign send.

<Warning>
  Stopping a test cannot be undone. Make sure you've gathered enough data before ending the experiment.
</Warning>
