The Semantiks Console dashboard gives enterprises real‑time visibility into conversations led by AI agents across all connected channels. Its design supports two complementary objectives: understand system‑level behavior (volumes, duration, engagement) and quickly drill into any single session for evidence‑based decisions (retuning prompts, updating knowledge, or handing issues to operations). Start with the high‑level structure to frame what you're seeing, then use the right‑side panel to read details without losing your place.

## Overview of the Dashboard

When you access the **Conversations Dashboard**, you will see:

- **KPIs (Key Performance Indicators):** High-level metrics summarizing performance.
- **Conversations Table:** A detailed list of all conversations with users.
- **Conversation Panel:** A right-side panel that shows more details when you click on a conversation.

---

## KPIs Cards

The top section provides at-a-glance metrics:

- **Total conversations** – The number of conversations in the selected time range.
- **Average duration** – The average length of conversations.
- **User messages** – Average number of messages sent by users.
- **Agent messages** – Average number of messages sent by the agent.

<Note>Green arrows indicate growth compared to the previous period, while red arrows indicate a decrease.</Note>
<Tip>You can filter the time range by selecting **Today, 7D, 30D, or Year**.</Tip>
---

## Conversations Table

Below the KPIs, you will find a table with all recorded conversations. Each row represents a conversation with details such as:

- **Channel:** The communication channel used (e.g., Freshchat Bubble).  
- **Profile Name:** Name of the user (if captured by the channel).  
- **External ID:** User ID from the channel.  
- **Last Message:** Preview of the most recent message in the conversation.  
- **Number of Messages:** Total messages exchanged (with the AI agent).  
- **Start:** Date and time when the conversation started.  
- **Control:** Indicates who ended the conversation (agent or human).  
- **Status:** Current status of the conversation (Active, Ended).  
- **Tag:** Labels or categories assigned to the conversation.  
- **Bubble:** Opens the **Conversation Panel** for more details.

### Filters & Search

The dashboard allows you to quickly find relevant conversations:

- **Search Bar:** Search by conversation content, external user ID, or messages.
- **Filters:**
  - **Channel** – Filter by the communication channel (e.g., Bubble, Playground, WhatsApp Meta, Freshchat, Zendesk, etc.).
  - **Status** – Active or Ended conversations.
  - **Control** – Agent-controlled or human-controlled conversations.
  - **Tags** – Filter by specific tags assigned to conversations.
  - **Sentiment** – Positive, neutral, or negative user sentiment.
  - **Topic** – Filter by auto-classified conversation topics.
  - **Task Success** – Success, partial success, failure, or undetermined.
  - **CSAT** – Set a minimum and maximum CSAT score (1–5).
  - **Message Count** – Filter by the number of messages exchanged.

- **Action Buttons:**
  - **Manage Tags** – Opens the tag management modal to create or edit tags.
  - **Clear Filters** – Resets all filters to their default values.
  - **Refresh** – Fetches the latest conversations from the server.

### Metadata Filters

Below the standard filter bar, a collapsible **Metadata Filters** section lets you narrow down conversations based on **user metadata** sent by your integrated channels. This is especially useful when your channels pass custom user information (such as customer IDs, emails, or account identifiers) alongside conversations.

#### How it works

1. **Expand the section** – Click the **Metadata Filters** header to reveal the available metadata fields. A small badge shows how many metadata filters are currently active.
2. **Channels & fields** – Metadata fields are grouped by channel (e.g., *Freshchat Bubble*, *WhatsApp Meta*). Each channel card lists the metadata fields that have been detected in conversations for your agent. Only channels that have sent metadata will appear.
3. **Filter by value** – Type a value into any field's text input to filter conversations where that metadata field matches your query.
4. **Clear per channel** – Click the **✕** button on a channel header to clear all metadata filters for that channel at once.
5. **Collapse channels** – Click the arrow on a channel header to expand or collapse its field list, keeping the filter bar tidy.

<Tip>
Metadata filters are especially powerful when combined with standard filters. For example, filter by a specific **Channel** and then add a metadata filter for `customer_id` to find all conversations from a particular customer.
</Tip>

<Note>
The metadata fields shown depend on what your integrated channels send. If no metadata has been received for any channel, the section will display an informational message. Adding metadata to your channel integration is typically done on the integration side (e.g., passing custom attributes via Freshchat or Zendesk).
</Note>

### Conversation Detail Panel

Clicking on a row opens the **Conversation Panel** on the right side. Here, you can:

- **Read the full conversation** between the user and the agent.
- **See conversation metadata**: channel, start time, status, and tags.
- **Review the summary** and resolution details.

---

## Best Practices

- **Use date filters** to focus on the most relevant time period for your analysis.
- **Combine multiple filters** (channel + sentiment + topic) to narrow down to the conversations that matter most.
- **Use metadata filters** to trace specific customers or user attributes across conversations.
- **Review tagged conversations** to understand common patterns and improve agent responses.
- **Export data** for external reporting using the export options available in the table toolbar.
