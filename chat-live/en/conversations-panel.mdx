---
title: "Conversations Panel"
description: "Manage live conversations between your AI agent, your team members, and end users in real time."
---

The **Conversations Panel** is the central hub of the Contact Center. It gives your team a real-time view of every active conversation your AI agent is handling, and lets members step in, take over, or reassign conversations as needed.

## Conversation list

On the left side of the panel you'll see a list of all conversations, organized by status:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Active** | The conversation is ongoing — either handled by the AI agent or by a human member |
| **Closed** | The conversation has ended (resolved or timed out) |

Click any conversation to open its detail view on the right.

## Conversation detail

When you select a conversation, you'll see:

- **Message thread** — the full exchange between the user, the AI agent, and any human members who participated
- **Contact info** — name, channel, external ID, and any memories collected
- **Conversation metadata** — status, assigned member, channel, timestamps

## Taking a conversation

When the AI agent is handling a conversation, any team member can **take** it to respond manually. Taking a conversation pauses the AI agent and lets the member reply directly.

**Who can take a conversation:**

- Any member can take an **unassigned** conversation (one that has no member assigned to it).
- If a conversation is **assigned to you**, you can take it.
- If a conversation is **assigned to another member**, you cannot take it directly — only the assigned member can.

<Note>
The "Take conversation" button is only available when you are allowed to take the conversation based on the rules above.
</Note>

## Assigning & passing conversations

You can assign an active, agent-handled conversation to any available team member using the **Assign** dropdown. This is useful for routing conversations to the right person based on expertise or availability.

**Key behavior:**

- **Any member** can use the assign dropdown to pass a conversation to another team member, even if the conversation is already assigned to someone else.
- If you **cannot take** the conversation yourself (because it is assigned to another member), you will not appear in the assignable members list — but you can still pass it to others.
- When a conversation is **pre-assigned** (assigned to a member but still being handled by the AI agent), the assigned member sees a "Take conversation" call-to-action to step in.

<Tip>
Use conversation assignment to coordinate handoffs between team members. For example, a first-line support agent can pass a technical question to a specialist without needing admin privileges.
</Tip>

## Handling a conversation

Once a member takes a conversation:

1. The AI agent stops replying automatically.
2. The member can read the full history and reply directly.
3. When finished, the member can **return the conversation to the AI agent** or **close** it.

## Returning to the AI agent

If a member decides the AI agent should continue handling the conversation, they can return it. The agent picks up where it left off, using the full conversation context.

## Closing a conversation

Members can manually close a conversation when the issue is resolved. Closed conversations move to the **Closed** tab and are available for review in the conversation history.

## Key notes

- Any team member can pass conversations to other members, regardless of their role.
- Only unassigned conversations or conversations assigned to you can be taken directly.
- The AI agent remains aware of the full conversation history, even after a human member intervenes and returns control.
