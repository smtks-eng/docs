The **Contact Center Dashboard** gives you a unified view of what is happening right now in your human-support operation and how it has performed over a configurable time window. Use it to balance agent workload in real time and to spot trends that inform staffing and process decisions.

## Live section

The top of the page displays four real-time metric cards that refresh automatically every 30 seconds:

| Card | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Available agents** | Number of agents currently online and available to take conversations. Displayed in teal. |
| **Customers waiting** | Number of customers currently in the queue waiting for a human agent. |
| **Active conversations** | Number of conversations being handled by human agents right now. |
| **Longest wait** | The elapsed time of the oldest conversation still waiting in the queue. Displayed in amber. |

A **Live** badge next to the section title confirms the data is being polled continuously.

<Tip>
Keep this page open on a shared screen so the team can see queue pressure at a glance and jump in when customers are waiting.
</Tip>

## Windowed KPIs

Below the live cards you will find historical KPIs scoped to a selectable time window (**Today, 7 D, 30 D, Year**, or a custom date range).

### Conversations

- **Escalation rate** — percentage of AI conversations that were escalated to a human agent.
- **Total escalated** — absolute count of escalated conversations.
- **Peak day** — the single day with the most escalated conversations and its count.

### Origin breakdown

A ranked list showing where escalated conversations came from (e.g., WhatsApp, Freshchat Bubble) so you can see which channels generate the most human-support demand.

### Wait-time & duration

- **Avg wait time** — average time customers spent in the queue before a human agent responded.
- **Avg handle time** — average duration of human-handled conversations.
- **First-response-time (FRT)** — time from the moment a conversation enters the queue to the first human reply.

<Note>
KPI cards show a comparison arrow (green ▲ / red ▼) relative to the previous period of the same length so you can tell at a glance whether things are improving.
</Note>

## Member connection status

To see which team members are currently connected or disconnected, go to **Settings → Organization → Members**. Admins and owners will see a colored dot next to each member's name:

- 🟢 **Green dot** — the member is connected.
- ⚫ **Gray dot** — the member is disconnected.

The dots update automatically every 30 seconds without requiring a page reload.

<Tip>
Use the member connection indicators alongside the live dashboard to understand whether available capacity matches current queue pressure.
</Tip>
