The **Escalations Board** gives your team a real-time operational view of every conversation that has been escalated to a human agent. Instead of switching between the inbox and separate reports, supervisors and agents can see at a glance which customers are waiting, who is handling what, and whether response-time targets are being met.

You can find the Escalations Board under **Contact Center > Live Chat > Escalations Board** in the left sidebar.

---

## KPI Cards

At the top of the board, four cards summarize the current state of escalations (based on the last 24 hours):

| KPI | What it measures |
|---|---|
| **Open** | Number of escalated conversations that have not yet been resolved |
| **Awaiting** | Number of escalated conversations still waiting for the first human reply |
| **Overdue** | Number of awaiting conversations that have exceeded the configured response-time threshold |
| **Median First Response** | The median time between escalation and the first human message, across all conversations that have received a reply |

<Tip>
The **Overdue** count turns red when there are conversations past the threshold, helping you spot SLA breaches immediately.
</Tip>

---

## Conversations Table

Below the KPIs you'll find a table listing all escalated conversations. Each row shows:

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| **Conversation** | Customer name and a short summary of the conversation |
| **Channel** | The channel the conversation originated from (e.g., Chat Widget, WhatsApp) |
| **Status** | Current board status: **Awaiting** (no human reply yet), **Attended** (human has replied), or **Resolved** (conversation ended) |
| **Assignee** | The team member assigned to the conversation, or an **Assign** button if unassigned |
| **Waiting** | Time elapsed between escalation and first human reply. Displays in red if it exceeds the threshold |

Click any row to open the **detail drawer** for that conversation.

### Searching and Filtering

- **Search bar** — Filter conversations by customer name or conversation ID.
- **Attention filter tabs** — Quickly switch between:
  - **All** — every escalated conversation
  - **Awaiting** — only conversations waiting for a first human reply
  - **Overdue** — only conversations that have exceeded the response-time threshold

### Overdue Threshold

The overdue threshold determines how many minutes can pass before an awaiting conversation is flagged as overdue. You can adjust it using the **−** and **+** buttons in the toolbar.

- **Default:** 15 minutes
- **Range:** 5 – 120 minutes (in 5-minute increments)

Conversations that exceed this threshold are tagged with an **Overdue** label. Conversations that were eventually replied to but after the threshold show an **Answered late** label instead.

## Detail Drawer

Clicking a conversation row opens a side panel with full context:

### Identification

- **Conversation ID** — A truncated ID with a copy-to-clipboard button.
- **Customer ID** — The end user's phone number or external user ID.
- **Channel** — The channel name.

### Assignee

Shows the currently assigned team member (with their avatar and active conversation count) or an **Unassigned** indicator. From here you can:

- **Assign / Reassign** — Open the assignment dialog to pick a team member.
- **Return to AI** — Unassign the conversation and hand it back to the AI agent.

<Note>
Regular team members can only assign conversations to themselves. **Admins and Owners** can assign or reassign conversations to any available team member.
</Note>

### Timeline

A visual timeline traces the lifecycle of the escalation:

1. **Conversation started** — When the conversation was created, and on which channel.
2. **Escalated** — When the conversation was escalated to a human.
3. **First human reply** — When a human agent first responded, including whether the reply was within or over the configured threshold.
4. **Resolved** — When the conversation ended (if applicable).

### Open Conversation

At the bottom of the drawer, the **Open conversation** button takes you directly to the conversation in the Live Chat inbox for full messaging.

---

## Assigning Conversations

When you click **Assign** (on an unassigned row or from the detail drawer), a dialog lists all team members in your organization:

- Members are sorted with **available** members first.
- Each entry shows the member's name and availability status (**Available now** or **Unavailable**).
- Only available members display an **Assign** button.
- Regular members only see themselves in the list; admins and owners see everyone.

After assigning, the board refreshes automatically to reflect the change.

---

## Permissions

| Role | Can do |
|---|---|
| **Owner / Admin** | View the board, assign or reassign any conversation to any member, return conversations to AI |
| **Member** | View the board, assign unassigned conversations to themselves, return their own conversations to AI |

---

## Tips for Using the Board

<Tip>
Keep the board open during business hours so your team can react to new escalations as they arrive. Use the **Overdue** filter to prioritize conversations that have been waiting the longest.
</Tip>

<Tip>
Adjust the overdue threshold to match your internal SLA. For example, if your team targets a 10-minute first response, set the threshold to 10 minutes so overdue flags align with your actual targets.
</Tip>

<Tip>
Use the **Median First Response** KPI to track your team's responsiveness over the day. If it starts climbing, consider redistributing workload or bringing in additional agents.
</Tip>
