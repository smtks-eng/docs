title: "Escalations Board"
description: "Monitor and manage escalated conversations in real time—assign agents, track response times, and resolve issues from a single board."
---

The **Escalations Board** gives managers a real-time Kanban-style view of every conversation that has been escalated from the AI agent to a human. Use it to monitor open cases, reassign conversations, and keep response times under control.

<Note>
Escalations Board is a **Beta** feature. Some capabilities may evolve as the feature matures.
</Note>

## Access requirements

The Escalations Board is restricted to **organization owners and admins**. Members with a regular "member" role will see a *Restricted access* message instead of the board. If you believe you need access, ask an organization owner or admin to update your role.

## Date range filter

At the top of the board you will find a **date range filter** that controls which escalations are loaded:

- **Preset pills** — quick-select buttons for common windows: **1d**, **7d**, **10d**, **15d**, and **30d**. The default is **7d**.
- **Custom range picker** — click the calendar icon to choose an arbitrary start and end date.

When you select a preset, any custom range is cleared and vice versa. The KPI cards and the table below automatically refresh to reflect the chosen period.

## KPI cards

Four cards at the top summarize the current state of escalations within the selected date range:

| Card | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Open** | Conversations that are not yet resolved |
| **Awaiting** | Conversations waiting for a human to respond |
| **Median first response** | The median time from escalation to the first human reply |
| **Overdue** | Conversations that have exceeded the configured response-time threshold |

## Attention filter

Below the KPIs, a set of quick-filter pills lets you narrow the table:

- **All** — every escalation in the selected date range
- **Awaiting** — only conversations still waiting for a human reply
- **Overdue** — only conversations past the response-time threshold

You can also type in the **search bar** to find a specific conversation by customer name or conversation ID.

## Escalation table

The table lists one row per escalated conversation with the following columns:

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| **Conversation** | The customer's name (or identifier) |
| **Channel** | The channel where the conversation originated |
| **Status** | Current board status — *Awaiting*, *Attended*, or *Resolved* |
| **Assignee** | The team member assigned to the conversation (if any) |
| **Waiting** | How long the conversation has been waiting for a response |

### Sorting columns

Every column header is clickable. Click once to sort **ascending**, click again to sort **descending**, and click a third time to **remove the sort**. A small arrow icon indicates the active sort column and direction.

- **Conversation** — alphabetical by customer name
- **Channel** — alphabetical by channel label
- **Status** — ordered by urgency: Awaiting → Attended → Resolved
- **Assignee** — alphabetical by assignee name; unassigned rows always appear last regardless of sort direction
- **Waiting** — by wait time in minutes

<Tip>
Sort by **Waiting** descending to surface the conversations that have been waiting the longest and may need immediate attention.
</Tip>

## Conversation detail panel

Click any row to open a slide-over panel with full details:

- **Conversation timeline** — key events from escalation to resolution, including when the first human message was sent and who sent it
- **Assign / Reassign** — assign the conversation to yourself or another team member
- **Mark as resolved** — close the escalation once the issue is handled
- **Copy conversation ID** — for referencing in other tools
- **Open full conversation** — jump to the conversation in the Live Chat panel

## Response-time threshold

You can configure the **response-time threshold** (in minutes) that defines when a conversation is considered *overdue*. Conversations that exceed this threshold are highlighted and counted in the Overdue KPI.

## Key notes

- The board auto-refreshes when you change the date range, but you can also trigger a manual refresh.
- Only conversations escalated within the selected date range are shown.
- Reassigning a conversation notifies the new assignee (if notifications are enabled).
- The board is **read-only for resolved conversations** — you can review them but cannot change their status.
