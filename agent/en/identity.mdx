Your agent's identity is what shapes the **first impression** users get when interacting with it. This section allows you to define how your agent presents itself—from name and greeting to tone, language, and availability. Setting up your agent's identity helps deliver a more human, consistent, and trustworthy experience.

<Info>
Don't forget to click **Save changes** after editing any field in the Identity section. Otherwise, your updates won't be saved.
</Info>

### General Information

In the **General** tab, you can upload a profile image, assign a name to your agent, and define how it greets and says goodbye to users.

<Note>
  The **profile image** should be square (1:1 ratio) and no larger than 2MB.
</Note>

![identity_agent-step-1.png](/images/agent/identity_agent/identity_agent-step-1.png)

- **Agent name**: This is the display name of your agent. Choose something friendly and aligned with your brand.
- **Agent greeting**: Define the message your agent uses to welcome users. This is the first thing they'll read.
- **Agent farewell**: Enter the closing message your agent will send at the end of the conversation.
- **Special communication instructions**: This is where you guide your agent on how to interact. Include tone, formal/informal treatment, and any specific behavior.
- **Agent time zone**: Select the correct time zone for your agent. This is essential for time-based features like scheduled greetings or availability settings.

![identity_agent-step-2.png](/images/agent/identity_agent/identity_agent-step-2.png)

<Tip>
  Use this section to make your agent feel truly personal and brand-aligned. Be clear, but also warm.
</Tip>

### Language

In the **Language** tab, you can configure the languages your agent understands and the way it communicates.

![identity_agent-step-3.png](/images/agent/identity_agent/identity_agent-step-3.png)

- **Languages**: Choose one or more supported languages. Multilingual agents can switch automatically depending on the user's language.

  ![identity_agent-step-4.png](/images/agent/identity_agent/identity_agent-step-4.png)
- **Style**: Choose how the agent expresses ideas—e.g., Creative, Persuasive, Concise, or Playful.

  ![identity_agent-step-5.png](/images/agent/identity_agent/identity_agent-step-5.png)
- **Tone**: Set the emotional tone of your agent. Options include Professional, Empathetic, Casual, or Friendly.

  ![identity_agent-step-6.png](/images/agent/identity_agent/identity_agent-step-6.png)

<Tip>
  Mix and match styles and tones based on your brand personality and user expectations.
</Tip>

### Behaviour Instructions

The **Behaviour Instructions** tab lets you define short, specific directives that steer how your agent behaves during conversations. Think of them as granular rules that complement the general communication instructions you set in the General tab.

While the General tab's "Special communication instructions" field is great for broad guidance (tone, formality, etc.), behaviour instructions let you add **targeted rules** that can be individually managed, enabled, or disabled without touching the rest of your agent's configuration.

<Note>
  You can have up to **20 active** behaviour instructions at a time. Each instruction can be up to **350 characters** long.
</Note>

#### Creating a behaviour instruction

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Behaviour Instructions tab">
    Navigate to **AI Agent > Design > Identity** and select the **Behaviour Instructions** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add instruction">
    Click the **+ Add instruction** button in the toolbar. A side panel will open.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your instruction">
    Enter a short, clear directive in the **Instruction** field. For example:
    - *Always respond in a concise, friendly tone.*
    - *Never share pricing information—redirect the user to the sales team.*
    - *When the user asks about returns, always mention the 30-day policy.*
    
    A character counter shows how many characters you have left (max 350).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the enabled toggle">
    Use the **Enabled** toggle to decide whether this instruction is active immediately. You can also create it in a paused state and enable it later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save** to create the instruction. It will appear in your instructions table.
  </Step>
</Steps>

#### Managing behaviour instructions

The instructions table shows all your behaviour instructions with their current status (**Active** or **Paused**). From here you can:

- **Search** — use the search bar to find instructions by text.
- **Filter by status** — use the **All**, **Active**, or **Paused** tabs to narrow the list.
- **Toggle enabled/disabled** — use the switch on each row to quickly enable or disable an instruction without opening the editor.
- **Edit** — click the pencil icon to open the side panel and modify the instruction text or enabled state.
- **Delete** — click the trash icon to remove an instruction. You'll be asked to confirm before it's permanently deleted.

The toolbar also displays a counter showing how many instructions are active out of the maximum (e.g. `3/20`).

<Warning>
  Deleting a behaviour instruction is permanent and cannot be undone.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  Start with a handful of high-impact instructions and test them in the **Playground** before enabling more. This makes it easier to see the effect of each rule on your agent's responses.
</Tip>

#### Active instruction limit

If you already have **20 active** instructions and try to enable another one, the system will show an error message asking you to disable an existing instruction first. You can still create new instructions in a **paused** state—the limit only applies to active (enabled) instructions.
