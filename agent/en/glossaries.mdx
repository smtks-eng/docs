**Glossaries** let you define collections of terms and their definitions that your AI agent can reference during conversations. This ensures your agent uses the correct terminology for your industry, product, or organization — every time.

Glossaries are especially useful when your business has specific jargon, branded terms, acronyms, or technical vocabulary that the agent needs to understand and use accurately.

<Info>
  Glossaries live inside the **Knowledge** section of your agent. Navigate to **AI Agent > Design > Knowledge > Glossaries** tab to get started.
</Info>

---

## Core concepts

### Glossaries
A **glossary** is a named collection of term-definition pairs. You can create multiple glossaries to organize terms by category — for example, one glossary for product names, another for industry terms, and another for internal acronyms.

### Entries
An **entry** is a single term-definition pair inside a glossary. Each entry has:
- **Term** — the word or phrase you want the agent to recognize
- **Definition** — the meaning or explanation the agent should use when referencing that term

---

## The Glossaries interface

The Glossaries tab is organized into two main areas:

### Sidebar
The left sidebar lists all your glossaries and provides quick navigation:
- **All Glossaries** — view entries from every glossary at once
- **Individual glossaries** — click any glossary name to filter and view only its entries
- **Add glossary (+)** — create a new glossary directly from the sidebar
- The sidebar can be **collapsed** to give more room to the main content area

### Main panel
The main content area shows your glossary entries, grouped by glossary when viewing "All". From here you can:
- **Search** entries by term or definition using the search bar
- **Add entries** manually using the "Add new entry" button at the bottom of each glossary group
- **Edit entries** by clicking the pencil icon on any entry
- **Delete entries** by clicking the trash icon on any entry
- **Import entries from a document** using the "From document" button in the toolbar

## Creating a glossary

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Glossaries tab">
    Navigate to **AI Agent > Design > Knowledge** and select the **Glossaries** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the + button">
    In the sidebar, next to **All Glossaries**, click the **+** button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name your glossary">
    Enter a descriptive name for the glossary (e.g. `Product Terms`, `Medical Vocabulary`, `Company Acronyms`) and click **Save**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Choose clear, descriptive names for your glossaries. This makes it easier to find and manage terms as your knowledge base grows.
</Tip>

---

## Adding entries manually

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a glossary">
    In the sidebar, click the glossary where you want to add an entry — or stay on **All Glossaries** and use the "Add new entry" button under any group.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the entry dialog">
    Click **Add new entry**. A dialog will open.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the details">
    - **Glossary** — select or confirm which glossary this entry belongs to
    - **Term** — enter the word or phrase
    - **Definition** — provide the meaning or explanation
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save** to add the entry to your glossary.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Editing an entry

Click the **pencil icon** on any entry to open the edit dialog. You can update the term, definition, or move the entry to a different glossary. Click **Save** when you're done.

---

## Deleting entries and glossaries

### Deleting an entry
Click the **trash icon** on any entry. You'll be asked to confirm the deletion.

### Deleting a glossary
When viewing a specific glossary, click the **Delete glossary** button in the header bar. You can also delete a glossary from the "All" view by clicking the trash icon next to the glossary group header.

<Warning>
  Deleting a glossary removes it **and all the entries it contains**. This action cannot be undone.
</Warning>

## Importing entries from a document

You can automatically generate glossary entries from an uploaded file. The platform uses AI to extract terms and definitions from your document.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click 'From document'">
    In the toolbar at the top of the Glossaries tab, click the **From document** button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload a file">
    Select a file from your computer. Supported formats are **PDF**, **DOCX**, **TXT**, and **CSV**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the preview">
    After processing, a preview dialog shows all the extracted terms and definitions, organized by glossary. Each entry has a checkbox so you can select or deselect individual items.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select entries to import">
    Use the checkboxes to choose which entries you want to keep. You can also use **Select all** / **Deselect all** to quickly toggle all entries.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the import">
    Click **Save** to create the glossaries and entries. New glossaries will be created automatically if they don't already exist. If a glossary with the same name already exists, entries will be added to it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The AI extraction works best with well-structured documents that contain clear term-definition patterns, such as glossary pages, data dictionaries, or FAQ-style content.
</Note>

---

## Searching and filtering

- Use the **search bar** at the top to find entries by term or definition. The search is accent-insensitive and ignores punctuation.
- Use the **sidebar** to filter entries by glossary. Click a specific glossary to see only its entries, or click **All Glossaries** to see everything.

---

## Tips for effective glossaries

<Tip>
  - **Be specific** in your definitions. Include context that helps the agent understand when and how to use each term.
  - **Keep terms atomic.** One term per entry — avoid combining multiple concepts.
  - **Use multiple glossaries** to organize terms by domain or category.
  - **Review imported entries** carefully before saving. AI extraction is helpful but may need manual adjustments.
</Tip>
