**Glossaries** let you define the specific terms, jargon, and vocabulary your AI agent should know. Each glossary is a named collection of **term / definition** pairs that help the agent understand and use domain-specific language accurately.

This is especially useful when your business has internal terminology, product names, acronyms, or industry-specific concepts that a general-purpose language model might not know or might confuse.

<Info>
  You can access Glossaries from the **Knowledge** page by clicking the **Glossaries** tab.
</Info>

---

## How Glossaries Work

Glossaries are organized as named groups. Inside each glossary you add **entries** — each entry is a **term** paired with its **definition**. The agent uses these entries as reference material when responding to users, ensuring it uses the correct meaning and phrasing for your domain.

---

## Creating a Glossary

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Glossaries">
    Go to **AI Agent > Design > Knowledge** and select the **Glossaries** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a new glossary">
    In the left sidebar, click **Add Glossary**. Enter a name for your glossary (e.g. `Product Terms`, `Medical Vocabulary`, `Internal Acronyms`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the glossary">
    Click **Save** to create the glossary. You can now start adding entries.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Use descriptive glossary names so your team can quickly identify which domain or topic each glossary covers.
</Tip>

---

## Managing Glossary Entries

### Adding an entry manually

1. Select a glossary from the sidebar (or choose **ALL** to view entries across all glossaries).
2. Click **Add New Entry**.
3. Fill in the **Term** and **Definition** fields.
4. Click **Save**.

### Editing an entry

Click on any existing entry in the table to open the **Edit Entry** panel. Update the term or definition and click **Save**.

### Deleting an entry

Click the delete action on an entry row. A confirmation dialog will appear:

<Warning>
  Deleting an entry removes it permanently. This action cannot be undone.
</Warning>

### Searching entries

Use the **search bar** at the top of the entries table to filter entries by term or definition. The placeholder reads _"Search terms or definitions..."_.

## Importing Entries from a File

You can bulk-import glossary entries by uploading a file. Semantiks will automatically extract terms and definitions for you.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Import from file">
    Inside the Glossaries tab, click **Import from file**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload your file">
    Select a document containing the terms and definitions you want to import.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the import preview">
    An **Import Preview** dialog will appear showing all extracted entries. You can:
    - **Select all** or **Deselect all** entries
    - Individually check or uncheck entries you want to include
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save selected entries">
    Click **Save** to add the selected entries to your glossary.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The import process uses AI to extract term-definition pairs from your document. Review the preview carefully to ensure accuracy before saving.
</Note>

---

## Editing or Deleting a Glossary

You can rename a glossary by clicking **Edit Glossary** in the sidebar.

To delete an entire glossary, use the delete option on the glossary in the sidebar. A confirmation dialog will warn you:

<Warning>
  Deleting a glossary removes the glossary and **all entries** it contains. This action cannot be undone.
</Warning>

---

## Filtering by Glossary

The sidebar lists all your glossaries. Click any glossary name to filter the entries table to that glossary only. Click **ALL** to see entries from every glossary in a single view.

---

## Best Practices

- **Keep definitions concise** — the agent uses them as quick reference, not as full articles.
- **Group related terms** — create separate glossaries for different domains (e.g. one for product names, another for compliance terms).
- **Update regularly** — as your business evolves, make sure your glossaries stay current so the agent always has accurate terminology.
- **Use the import feature** for large term lists — it's much faster than adding entries one by one.
